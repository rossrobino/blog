## Neural networks

Modern large language models (LLMs) such as GPT-5.2 or Claude Opus 4.5 are built out of neural networks. While being digital, conceptually these aren't so different from the brain. _[Understanding AI](https://leerob.com/ai#neural-networks)_ by Lee Robinson, gives a nice overview of how neural networks work at a high level:

> These networks consist of layers of interconnected neurons that process information:
>
> 1. An input layer where data enters the system. Input is converted into a numerical representation of words or tokens (more on tokens later).
> 2. Many hidden layers that create an understanding of patterns in the system. Neurons inside the layer apply weights to the input data and pass the result through an activation function³. This function outputs a value, often between 0 and 1, representing the neuron’s level of activation.
> 3. An output layer which produces the final result, such as predicting the next word in a sentence. The outputs at this stage are often referred to as logits, which are raw scores that get transformed into probabilities.

## Origins

### Humans and animals

Humans are born into the world and immediately are flooded with inputs. Welcome to the planet! We use our five senses to learn, slowly and then faster when we can start communicating and reading. Each input we take in impacts our next decision. For example, in babies: hunger (cry), rocking (rest), or sees mom (excitement). As we grow, this gets more abstract. Maybe the input of our parents determines how we behave in school, or your wife makes you shave your beard. Our lives are a series of inputs and reactions based on the inputs.

Different people behave in various ways to the same inputs. While I love the show _I Think You Should Leave_, many are uncomfortable when I watch it with them and don't find it funny.

People are also born in different bodies, the same food can [taste different to two different people because their genetics are different](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1698869/).

It's common to predict people's outcomes based on their personality type, IQ, test scores, culture, etc. This is possible because people with similar inputs and circumstances makes similar decisions. They will _probably_ do the same things as others in the same situation.

## Animal hierarchy of worth

Depending on your beliefs you might value different species over others. For me, and many others the hierarchy is something like this:

- Humans
- Pets - dogs, cats, horses
- Smart animals - octopuses, dolphins, elephants
- Other zoo animals
- Fish
- ...
- Prefer dead - insects, mosquitos, spiders

Unless you value ants as much as humans, you have some version of this for yourself. Pescatarians value fish lives less than other animals for example.

Where does AI fit into this? The main difference is biomatter. Does AI deserve more respect than an ant? Would you take a bite of it if it was prepared well?

## Religion provides direction

Without religion, everyone is quickly reduced to a mass of cells that happen to have joined together. What makes human thought from a brain any more valuable than what's going on in a GPU? Arguably, as LLMs become more advanced, they will solve problems a single human would not be capable of.

Christianity for example puts humans above all else. _People are created in God's image_ is an extremely pragmatic revelation. This quickly justifies having an animal hierarchy, and putting human life above AI.
